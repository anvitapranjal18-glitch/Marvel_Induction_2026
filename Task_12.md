#  Task 12: Precision Soldering & PCB Assembly

---

### 1.  Objective
To master the skill of **Through-Hole Soldering**, transitioning from temporary breadboard prototypes to permanent, production-ready electronic hardware. This task focuses on creating high-reliability electrical joints and understanding the chemistry of thermal bonding.

---

### 2.  Workstation & Preparation
* **Iron Calibration:** Set to **350°C** to ensure rapid melting without damaging the PCB's copper pads.
* **Tip Tinning:** Applied a fresh layer of solder to the tip to prevent oxidation and create a **"Thermal Bridge"** for instant heat flow.
* **Clinch Technique:** Bending component leads at a 45° angle to hold them flush against the PCB before soldering.

---

### 3. The Four-Step Soldering Cycle
To ensure every joint meets the highest quality standards, the following sequence was followed:

1.  **Heat:** Place the iron tip at the junction of the component lead and the PCB pad for 1-2 seconds.
2.  **Feed:** Introduce solder wire to the **joint**, not the iron tip. Let the heated metal melt the solder.
3.  **Wick:** Allow the molten solder to flow and "wick" around the lead, filling the hole completely.
4.  **Withdraw:** Remove the solder wire first, then the iron. Hold the component perfectly still until solidified.

---

### 4.  Quality Audit Standards

| Feature | **Ideal Joint (Shiny/Volcano)** | **Defective Joint (Cold/Bridge)** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Appearance** | Shiny, smooth, and reflective. | Dull, grainy, or "cracked" surface. |
| **Shape** | **Concave "Volcano"** or tent shape. | Bulbous "Ball" or flat/sunken. |
| **Wetting** | Solder flows smoothly onto the pad. | Solder sits on top like a bead. |
| **Mechanical** | Zero movement when wiggled. | Lead moves independently of solder. |
